## Keynote: AI Systems Engineering - a new engineering discipline? 
  
### Abstract:  
The future viability of up-to-now successful companies and pubic agencies largely depends on they are able to systematically integrate artificial intelligence (AI)
methods into their processes, products, and services, thereby achieving sustainable economic success. This talk discusses the need to align and/or update the established 
systems engineering methods with the rapidly evolving AI technologies, and poses the question if the engineering of AI systems will become a new engineering discipline on its own.

## Beyond Data Pipelines: Using Apache Airflow to Automate Autonomous Driving Tests 
  
### Abstract:  
Validating software for autonomous driving is one of the biggest challenges in modern automotive development. Ensuring the safety and reliability of these complex systems requires millions of test kilometers in a virtual environment. This, in turn, demands a robust, scalable, and highly automated testing infrastructure.

In this talk, we will present a real-world case study from Bosch on how we leverage the well-known open-source workflow management system, Apache Airflow, to master this exact challenge. Instead of using it for classic ETL processes, we use Airflow to orchestrate the entire testing lifecycle for autonomous driving functions in a virtual environment.

This presentation will cover:

1. The Problem: The complexity and sheer scale of testing required to validate and release autonomous driving functions.
2. The Solution: Why we chose Apache Airflow and how we adapted it to control thousands of parallel test scenarios, collect results, and generate reports.
3. The Lessons: The practical benefits and unexpected hurdles we encountered when applying an open-source tool in a safety-critical automotive environment.
4. The Contribution: Our experience goes beyond mere consumption. We will briefly touch upon how the demands of our use case led us to contribute fixes, improvements and even new features back to the Apache Airflow community.

This talk is intended for anyone interested in the practical application of open source in unconventional yet highly relevant industrial fields—from automotive experts and DevOps engineers to active open-source contributors.

## Beyond the Prompt: Technical Insights into LLMs for Software Vulnerability Detection 
  
### Abstract:  
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is fundamentally changing how we build software. While AI agents and Large Language Models (LLMs) boost productivity by writing code, they also introduce new risks. One challenge emerges in vulnerability management: AI-generated code often lacks understanding of the broader project and can be redundant, which makes it hard for traditional security tools (such as SAST) to identify vulnerabilities.
In this talk, we provide a technical overview of the "LLMs for Software Vulnerability Detection" landscape. We explore the current state of research, comparing different approaches, models, and advanced techniques, e.g., integrating knowledge bases and reasoning capabilities. Beyond the theory, we offer insights into the real effectiveness of current LLM-based approaches as well as best practices on how to integrate LLMs into development processes.

## Inside the OWASP Top 10 for Agentic AI: The New Security Reality 
  
### Abstract:  
Agentic AI systems are moving beyond simple prompt-response interactions. They plan, reason, maintain memory, invoke tools, access external systems, and act toward defined goals. This growing autonomy fundamentally reshapes the security landscape. 

The session introduces the OWASP GenAI Security Project, its mission, and its broader body of guidance for securing generative AI systems, before diving into the OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications 2026. The key risk categories identified by the community are examined in depth, along with the threat logic behind them and their impact on real-world agentic architectures. Special focus is given to how autonomy, tool integration, memory, and goal-driven behavior expand the attack surface and challenge traditional security assumptions. 

Attendees will leave with a clear understanding of the core risk categories in Agentic AI and a solid foundation for identifying and addressing the most relevant security risks in autonomous systems.

## Keynote: Trust has a Supply Chain - Open Standards and the Infrastructure of Trustworthy AI 
  
### Abstract:  
When an emergency manager asks an AI agent to assess flood risk, data from six organizations across multiple countries must be assembled in seconds. Every dataset carries assumptions about formats, models, and meaning. If any link in that chain is ambiguous, the AI's output looks plausible but can't be trusted. Drawing on OGC's AI-DGGS Disaster Management Pilot, this keynote shows why trustworthy AI requires trustworthy supply chains, and why open standards are the infrastructure that makes both possible. The parallels to software supply chains are striking: provenance, semantic clarity, and machine-readable metadata matter as much for data as for code. Trust isn't built by models. It's built on the open standards underneath.

## LF Agentic AI Foundation and OpenChain AI SBOM Guide 
  
### Abstract:  
Modern software consists of more than 70% open-source components. These building blocks power build pipelines, testing strategies, logging infrastructures, and runtime environments. They form the technical foundation of today’s business software. Starting in 2027, manufacturers of commercial software will bear full responsibility for these dependencies under the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA).
With Support & Care, we provide professional stewardship for precisely these foundational components within the Java ecosystem. Specifically, Support & Care covers a curated package of core Java technologies: Apache Maven, JUnit, Apache Log4j, Apache Commons, and the Eclipse Temurin Java Runtime. This is enabled through close collaboration with the respective maintainers and active engagement within the project communities.
In this presentation, we demonstrate why true digital sovereignty does not end with a cloud strategy, but continues with the active governance of business-critical open-source core components. Support & Care addresses exactly this layer and helps you to enable resilient, auditable, and CRA-compliant software supply chains.

### Bio  
Hendrik Ebbers is the founder of Open Elements, a company focused on professional open source services, governance, and sustainable software development. Open Elements is one of the main contributor of Apache Maven.

Hendrik leads the technical steering committee for the Hiero project and is Vice Chair of a Technical Advisory Board of Linux Foundation.
