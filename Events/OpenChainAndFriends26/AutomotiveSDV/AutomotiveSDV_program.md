## ISO5230 - OpenChain as a Negotiation Boost 
  
### Abstract:  
The presentation, "OpenChain as a Negotiation Boost," explores the gap between OpenChain's promise of streamlined OSS license compliance and security and the practical realities of negotiation. It highlights how a lack of awareness among negotiators, insufficient promotional materials, and missing of exemplary legal templates hinder OpenChain's adoption in negotiations and how this can be solved. We will also look at the customer perceptions and expectations beyond OpenChain's scope and the influence of external factors. It ends with an open discussion.  
  
*Christian von Drathen (Robert Bosch GmbH)*  
  
### Bio  
Christian von Drathen, LL.M. (University of Bristol)
Legal Counsel and Fachanwalt für Gewerblichen Rechtsschutz (Certified specialist lawyer for protection of industrial property rights and competition law)
I have been working in the field of IP for about 20 years, nearly 13 with Bosch.
My first contact with Open Source Software (OSS) in the legal field was the case of an IT retailer. I was a young lawyer and he was in the focus of Mr. McHardy. The dispute related to a device my client sold and that this would not come with the required OSS license fulfilment documents. Since then, I have been involved with OSS and law in many and diverse aspects. And many times, I could draw on the experience of my first case. One emphasis is creating and negotiating OSS contract terms in the supply chain. Which – inevitably – brought me to OpenChain…

## Your first steps in Open Source - working with Open Source Communities 
  
### Abstract:  
The automotive industry undergoes a profound transformation, driven by increasing software complexity. This shift demands unprecedented levels of collaboration and joint integration. The rapid growth of the Eclipse SDV working group illustrates this vividly. As we accelerate towards the software-defined vehicle, a critical question is how to run and grow the involved open source projects to deliver lasting value to the contributing organisations and the projects. 

While many of these effects of open collaboration are not unique to a particular domain, the lessons learned from other already more "software-defined" industries unlock invaluable insights. 
This talk will delve into the practical implementation and business benefits of embracing Open Source development, illustrated with concrete examples. 

We will explore how contributing to open source effectively contributes to reaching business goals, using insights from the TODO Group Business Guide. In most cases, this involves fostering a vibrant community and interacting with community members in a meaningful and respectful way. We then assemble a powerful toolkit of how communities have been created successfully in practice, inspired by the principles in Pieter Hintjens' book "Social Architecture". This leads us to the vital observation of what new competencies may become necessary to help the shift towards Open Source development. 

Join us to uncover enablers of sustainable Open Source development, learn from cross-industry successes, and equip your teams to thrive in the rapidly evolving software-defined world.  
  
*Sven Erik Jeroschewski (Robert Bosch GmbH)*  
  
### Bio  
Sven Erik Jeroschewski is a software engineer with Bosch Digital. As member of the Center of Excellence for Open Source and InnerSource he applies and extends Open Source technologies, especially from the Eclipse SDV working group, in a technically sound and compliant manner to the connected driving and industrial automation domains. Sven studied Computer Engineering at the TU Berlin and the University of Oklahoma. He is a committer with Eclipse Kuksa and Eclipse SDV Blueprints.

## ASF NuttX RTOS - introduction and benefits 
  
### Abstract:  
NuttX RTOS is a stable RTOS under the Apache Software Foundation and became a lead project in 2019. 
The project supports all major architectures and over 500 boards which provides a wide implementation coverage 
Multiple companies are using the RTOS to develop products that are close to us (ex fitness trackers, IOT), drive 
around us (Xiaomi car), fly around (PX4 drones, rockets) or event walk the moon (JAXAs moon robot LEM 1) 
The NuttX RTOS is built around POSIX compliance and provides easy portability for applications build for Linux. 
During the talk I will present the NuttX history, applications, portability and compliance which can make it 
attractive for your use-case 
  
  
*Alin Jerpela (Sony)*  
  
### Bio  
Alin Jerpelea is a senior software architect and a local OSPO member at Sony, located in Lund, Sweden. 
He has been part of Sony since 2010 when he joined Sony Mobile as a community manager for the Xperia Open Source initiative and has since worked in numerous projects and Open Source collaboration. 
Currently he is working together with the Apache NuttX community serving as Project Management Committee member, TODO Group as an Ambassador and Linux Open Chain working groups.
