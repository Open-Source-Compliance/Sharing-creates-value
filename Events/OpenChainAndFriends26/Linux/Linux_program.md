## Reproducibility > OSS > SBOM 
  
### Abstract:  
An SBOM tells you what is in your product. But not how it was built. 
Traditional "./configure && make && make install"workflows are inherently host-dependent: implicit toolchain versions, system headers, and environment state make builds difficult to reproduce and impossible to audit after the fact. 
The Yocto Project's metadata addresses this by capturing the entire build context: toolchains, configurations, patch series, dependency policies, everything is captured as versioned, shareable source. This moves compliance beyond component lists toward demonstrable 
provenance: not just what shipped, but how and why the binary looks the way it does. 
Meaningful open source compliance requires treating the build system as a first-class artifact. Yocto offers a practical model for what that looks like.

### Bio  
Josef has been active for more than 15 years as a "Complete"Stack developer for industrial controls doing everything from debugging hardware to writing drivers, from application development to web front ends. Then he decided to become a "tech people person"! A passion for showing, telling, and teaching people in both entertaining and engaging ways led Josef to Mender.io. Here, he tries to make the world better and more secure by enabling OTA updates for as many devices as possible. As a prolific public persona, Josef has also reached many thousands of aspiring developers not only through his presentations at various events and conferences but also by livecoding sessions and webinars. 
Taking his passion for connecting and empowering developers, combining it with a keen interest in building systems and how they can be brought to the next generation of developers, Josef has been awarded Ambassador status and is serving as Community Manager for the Yocto Project.
